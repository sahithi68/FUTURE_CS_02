## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing phishing emails and identifying security threats using professional tools and manual inspection techniques. Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, and banking details.

The goal of this project is to understand how phishing emails work, detect their indicators, and create awareness to prevent such attacks.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze phishing email samples
* Identify phishing indicators
* Perform email header analysis
* Classify emails based on risk levels
* Create awareness about phishing attacks
* Provide prevention guidelines

---

## 📧 Email Analysis Summary

* Analyzed **2 phishing email samples**
* Emails impersonated trusted services (e.g., PayPal)
* Used urgent subject lines to create panic
* Contained fake domains and suspicious links
* Failed authentication checks (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

* **Google Admin Toolbox** – Message Header Analyzer
* **MXToolbox** – Header Analyzer
* **Browser Inspection** – Manual link and domain checking

---

## 🔍 Analysis Approach

1. Collected phishing email samples
2. Extracted and analyzed email headers
3. Verified sender domain and IP address
4. Checked SPF, DKIM, and DMARC results
5. Identified phishing indicators
6. Classified risk level
7. Documented findings and prevention tips

---

## ⚠️ Phishing Indicators Identified

* Fake sender domain (e.g., fakepaypal.com)
* Urgent and fear-based language
* Blacklisted mail server
* Authentication failures (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
* Suspicious email structure
* Impersonation of trusted brands

---

## 🚨 Risk Classification

* **Safe:** Legitimate email with valid authentication
* **Low Risk:** Slightly suspicious but not harmful
* **Medium Risk:** Contains multiple warning signs
* **High Risk:** Confirmed phishing email

👉 **Final Result:**
All analyzed emails were classified as **High Risk Phishing Emails**

---

## 🛡️ Prevention Tips

* Do not click on suspicious links
* Always verify sender email address
* Use official websites directly
* Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
* Use spam filters and security tools
* Report phishing emails immediately

---

## ❌ Do’s and Don’ts

### ✅ Do’s

* Verify sender before taking action
* Report suspicious emails
* Stay alert while checking emails

### ❌ Don’ts

* Do not share passwords or OTPs
* Do not click unknown links
* Do not trust urgent messages blindly
